I exported the final design as a PNG and uploaded it to my GitHub repository.

## Task 4 – Professional Email and Etiquette Guide

I wrote two emails for this task. The first one was from a student requesting a deadline extension from a professor. I made sure to keep the tone respectful, explain the reason briefly without over-explaining, and end with a proper sign-off. The second email was to an internship coordinator expressing interest in a summer internship. I introduced myself, mentioned my branch and interests, and kept it concise because I know recruiters don't like long emails.

For the reflection part — I read about a case where a student posted an angry message about a faculty member on a college WhatsApp group. The message spread quickly outside the group and the student ended up facing a disciplinary hearing. What bothered me about this story is that the student probably didn't even think before sending it. If they had just sent a private, polite email instead, the whole situation could have been avoided. That's the thing about digital communication — once you send something, you can't take it back.

## Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

I chose UPI fraud as my topic because it felt the most relevant to my daily life. Almost everyone I know uses PhonePe or Google Pay, and I've heard stories of people getting scammed but never fully understood how it actually worked.

What surprised me the most while researching this topic was learning that receiving money on UPI never requires entering your PIN. That single piece of misinformation is responsible for so many people losing money. Scammers call victims saying "I'm sending you money, just enter your PIN to receive it" — and people believe them because it sounds logical. It's not. Your PIN is only ever needed when you send money.

The one habit I'm personally going to change is to stop using public Wi-Fi for anything related to banking or payments. I used to think it was fine as long as I was just checking my balance, but now I know that even that can be risky. Small habits like this can make a big difference.
