## What is UPI Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where
criminals trick victims into transferring money or revealing sensitive
payment credentials through deceptive means. It is one of the most
common cybercrimes targeting college students and young adults in India.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. The fraudster contacts the victim via phone, WhatsApp or social media,
   posing as a bank official, customer care agent or online seller.
2. They create a sense of urgency — claiming the victim's account will
   be blocked or that they have won a prize.
3. The victim is asked to share their UPI PIN, OTP or to scan a QR code
   to "verify" their account.
4. Once the victim shares the OTP or scans the QR code, money is
   immediately debited from their account.
5. The fraudster disconnects and becomes unreachable.

## Who is Usually Targeted?

- First-time UPI users and college students
- People selling items on platforms like OLX or Facebook Marketplace
- Job seekers who receive fake offer letters with "registration fee" demands
- Users who publicly share their UPI IDs on social media

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss, sometimes entire savings
- Emotional distress and loss of trust in digital payments
- Difficulty in recovering lost money as transactions are instant
- In severe cases, victims fall into debt or face academic disruption
